Joining and leaving the National Health Insurance scheme（国民健康保険の加入・脱退について）

Last updated date：2026/3/1

About the National Health Insurance scheme

Health insurance schemes provide partial or total coverage for medical expenses incurred in the course of examinations and treatment at medical institutions.
In Japan, there are the following health insurance schemes: "employee health insurance," which people sign up for through their workplace; the "Advanced Elderly Medical Service System," which people aged seventy-five and over sign up for; and "National Health Insurance," which everyone else signs up for.
In this case, "everyone else" refers to people who are not signed up for another health insurance scheme, which includes self-employed people, those working in the agricultural sector, retirees, etc.
If you are not signed up for employee health insurance through your workplace, the "Advanced Elderly Medical Service System," or receiving public assistance, please make sure to sign up for National Health Insurance at the municipality in which you live.
*Please inquire at your workplace for more information on employee health insurance. Please click the link for more information on the "Advanced Elderly Medical Service System".
*Non-Japanese residents are also required to sign up for health insurance if staying in Japan for more than 3 months.
*Foreign language guidebooks translated into English, Chinese, Korean, Spanish, and Portuguese are available on the website. Please visit the link to download a copy.

Who needs to sign up for health insurance?

There are the following health insurance schemes: "employee health insurance," which people sign up for through their workplace; the "Advanced Elderly Medical Service System," which people aged seventy-five and over sign up for; and "National Health Insurance," which everyone else signs up for.
Anyone who is not signed up for employee health insurance through their workplace, the "Advanced Elderly Medical Service System," or receiving public assistance is required by law to sign up for National Health Insurance.

About the Certificate of Eligibility and Notice of Eligibility Information
Depending on whether you are registered to use the Myna Health Insurance Card (IndividualNumber Card (My Number Card) registered for health insurance use), you will be issued either an “Notice of Eligibility Information” or an “Certificate of Eligibility.”:
・If you do not have a Myna Health Insurance Card: you will be issued a Certificate of Eligibility*1.
・If you have an Individual Number card: you will be issued a Notice of Eligibility Information*2.

*1 Certificate of Eligibility: You can show this to a hospital or other medical institution to receive treatment covered by health insurance.
*2 Notice of Eligibility Information: You can use this to check the insurance information that has been registered.

When you need to submit notification

If any of the following 3 items apply to you, you need to notify the National Health Insurance Section at the ward office of the ward in which you currently live within 14 days of applicability.
(Click on the item to check what you need to submit.)

(1) If joining the National Health Insurance scheme

  • If you leave your workplace's employee health insurance scheme or a National Health Insurance Society
  • If you move to Yokohama from outside the city
  • If you stop receiving public assistance
  • If you have a child

(2) If leaving the National Health Insurance scheme

  • If you join your workplace's employee health insurance scheme or a National Health Insurance Society
  • If you move out of Yokohama City
  • If you start receiving public assistance
  • If the health insurance cardholder dies

(3) Other

  • If you move in from another ward in Yokohama City
  • If you relocate within your current ward
  • If the head of household changes
  • If a household is split up or joined together
  • If your name changes
  • If you are going to live outside Yokohama City for study
  • If you have lost or damaged your health insurance card

Things you need when notifying that you have left your workplace's employee health insurance scheme or a National Health Insurance Society

  1. Documents that can confirm the date your previous health insurance coverage ended
    (Certificate of Loss of Health Insurance Eligibility, My Number Card with Myna Portal eligibility screen, etc.)
  2. A form of personal identification (resident card, passport, Individual Number card, driver's license, etc.)

Things you need when notifying that you have moved in from outside Yokohama City

  1. A form of personal identification (resident card, passport, Individual Number card, driver's license, etc.)

Things you need when notifying that you have stopped receiving public assistance

  1. Notification of revocation or suspension of public assistance
  2. A form of personal identification (resident card, passport, Individual Number card, driver's license, etc.)

Things you need when notifying that you have had a child

  1. Mother and Child Health Handbook
  2. A form of personal identification for the person coming to submit notification (resident card, passport, Individual Number card, driver's license, etc.)

Things you need when notifying that you joined your workplace's employee health insurance scheme or a National Health Insurance Society

  1. Documents that can confirm the date you obtained eligibility for your current health insurance
    (Certificate of Eligibility, Notice of Eligibility Information, Certificate of Health Insurance Eligibility Acquisition, My Number Card with Myna Portal eligibility screen, etc.)
  2. Certificate of Eligibility for National Health Insurance previously used
    *If you have moved or if the details on your Certificate of Eligibility, such as the name of the head of the household or the address, have changed, please bring the Certificate of Eligibility for all members of your household.

From the date you joined your workplace's health insurance scheme or National Health Insurance Society onward, you are no longer entitled to use your Certificate of Eligibility, etc. for City of Yokohama National Health Insurance.If you do, you may be asked to pay back any medical fees that the City of Yokohama has covered.

Things you need when notifying that you are moving out of Yokohama City

  1. Certificate of Eligibility for National Health Insurance previously used
    *If you have moved or if the details on your Certificate of Eligibility, such as the name of the head of the household or the address, have changed, please bring the Certificate of Eligibility for all members of your household.

From the day you move out of Yokohama City onward, you are no longer entitled to use your Certificate of Eligibility, etc. for City of Yokohama National Health Insurance. If you do, you may be asked to pay back any medical fees that the City of Yokohama has covered.

Things you need when notifying that you have started receiving public assistance

  1. Public Assistance Benefit Decision Notice
  2. Certificate of Eligibility for National Health Insurance previously used
    *If you have moved or if the details on your Certificate of Eligibility, such as the name of the head of the household or the address, have changed, please bring the Certificate of Eligibility for all members of your household.

Things you need when notifying that the health insurance cardholder has died

  1. Something certifying that the health insurance cardholder has died
  2. Certificate of Eligibility for National Health Insurance previously used
    *If you have moved or if the details on your Certificate of Eligibility, such as the name of the head of the household or the address, have changed, please bring the Certificate of Eligibility for all members of your household.

Things you need when notifying that you have moved in from another ward in Yokohama City

*Please submit notification at the ward office of the ward in which your new home is located.

  1. Certificate of Eligibility for National Health Insurance previously used in another ward
  2. A form of personal identification (resident card, passport, Individual Number card, driver's license, etc.)

Things you need when notifying that you have relocated within your current ward, the head of household has changed, your household has split up or joined together, or your name has changed

  1. Certificate of Eligibility for National Health Insurance previously used
    *If you have moved or if the details on your Certificate of Eligibility, such as the name of the head of the household or the address, have changed, please bring the Certificate of Eligibility for all members of your household.
  2. A form of personal identification (resident card, passport, Individual Number card, driver's license, etc.)

Things you need when notifying that you are going to live outside Yokohama City for study

  1. Student registration certificate

Things you need when notifying that you have lost or damaged your health insurance card

  1. In cases of loss: documents showing the insured person number, such as a notice of insurance premium determination or payment slips.In cases of damage: the damaged Certificate of Eligibility, etc.
  2. A form of personal identification (resident card, passport, Individual Number card, driver's license, etc.)
