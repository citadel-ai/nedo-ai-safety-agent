Taxes

Both foreigners and Japanese are required to pay various taxes.

Main taxes handled by the ward

There are two main types of taxes: national taxes paid to the national government and local taxes paid to the ward or metropolitan government. The main taxes handled by Edogawa Ward are as follows.

Special ward resident tax

Special ward resident tax and metropolitan resident tax, also known as inhabitant tax, are shared by residents of the area to cover the cost of ward services connected with their daily lives.

The following people are subject to special ward inhabitant tax

  • Persons who live in Edogawa Ward on January 1 of the current year and had income during the previous year
  • Those who had an office, business, or house in Edogawa Ward on January 1 of the current year, even if they do not currently live in Edogawa Ward.

Even those who currently have no income are subject to the tax if they had income (received salary) over a certain amount during the previous year.

Even if you move out of Edogawa Ward in the middle of the year, you will be taxed in the Edogawa Ward where you lived on January 1.

Amount of tax

The tax amount is the total of an income surcharge based on your income and a flat rate surcharge for those with income above a certain level.

Declaration

Those who are subject to taxation must report the amount of income for the previous year to the Edogawa Ward Office.

The deadline for filing is March 15 every year. (If it is a Saturday, Sunday, or holiday, it will be filed on the following weekday.)

Those who have filed a final income tax return or those whose only income during the previous year was employment income and whose employer has submitted a payroll report do not need to file a tax return.

Light Vehicle Tax

Tax is imposed on owners of light vehicles, motorcycles, etc. as of April 1 for the entire year.

Payment of taxes (payment)

A tax notice and payment slip will be sent to individuals in June for the special ward inhabitant tax and metropolitan inhabitant tax (for regular collection), and in May for the light motor vehicle tax. When paying the tax, please bring the payment slip to a financial institution such as a bank or post office, the Tax Payment Division of the Ward Office, the Ward Citizens Division, or any office or convenience store.

  • How to pay inhabitant tax
  • How to pay light motor vehicle tax

Payment by account transfer (for ordinary tax collection)

If you use account transfer, you do not need to go to a bank, post office, or ward office for each payment term (1st to 4th terms), and the payment will be automatically transferred from your account. Please fill out the "request form for account transfer (automatic payment)" available at each financial institution, affix your seal (registered seal), and submit it to the bank or post office.

  • Account transfer of inhabitant tax (special ward inhabitant tax and metropolitan inhabitant tax) and forest environment tax

Proof of Taxes

Proof of Taxes Issuable in Edogawa Ward

The tax certificates that can be issued by Edogawa Ward are as follows.

  • Proof of taxation and payment of special ward and metropolitan inhabitant taxes
  • Certificate of tax payment for light motor vehicle tax

In principle, this certificate will be issued to the person in question.

In the case of a proxy, a letter of attorney is required.
